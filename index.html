Expected Graduation: May 2028

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Kelley School of Business, Indiana University. My research lies at the intersection of Environmental and Energy Economics, Health Economics, and Industrial Organization. I use applied econometric methods to study how firms, markets, and policies shape outcomes in energy, transportation, and healthcare.

My current work examines topics including electric vehicle market dynamics, the adoption of solar energy by hospitals, physician expertise and technology diffusion, and the effects of auto franchise laws on EV adoption.
